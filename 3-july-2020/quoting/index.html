Elephants don’t belong in zoos. In the wild, they can roam up to 50 miles a day. And, they’re keen to sense danger:

A herd as far away as a hundred miles from a cull — the brutal practice of gunning down elephants in those areas where their numbers interfere with human settlements — can both emit and hear alarm calls outside our ears’ register about the unfolding cataclysm.
